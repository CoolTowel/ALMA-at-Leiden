About this project

ALMA , the Atacama Large Millimeter and submillimeter Array, is a radio telescope located in the Atacama Desert of northern Chile. It consists of 66 antennas but use the interferometry technology to combine their output to one image. ALMA helps us to reveal the secrets of the universe history, to discover our cosmic origin.

While the working tour, you will be introduced to some details of ALMA, the basic concepts of the Interferometry technique. It also contains some astronomy trivia, astronomy fun facts.

The largest extent of the ALMA array is comparable to the size of Leiden. The map below shows the 44 antennas' locations if they are located in the Leiden city.
