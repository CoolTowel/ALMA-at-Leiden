At the next antenna, a man sits beside the giant dish, with paper and pencil in hand. He stands up and greets the two as they arrived.

Erica introduces him to Robin: ‘This is Pascal Le Mans, our artist.’

‘An artist?’

‘Yes!’ Pascal responds. ‘I make the art for all the discoveries made at ALMA. Many of those beautiful astronomy images you see in the newspaper and on the internet... well, they're not real pictures.’

‘Really?’

‘No! Often, those are artist's impressions. Thanks to telescopes such as ALMA, we can find all kinds of far-off space objects. Stars, galaxies, exoplanets, all in deep space. But ALMA can't always tell us what they look like.’

Robin turns to Erica. ‘I don't understand. I thought you said ALMA created images?’

‘Oh, they do,’ Pascal says. ‘Just not the beautiful, colourful, striking kind. That's my job.’
