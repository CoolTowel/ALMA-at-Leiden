‘Can’t we just use one telescope?’ Robin asks.

‘Unfortunately not,’ Erica responds, ‘each telescope has an antenna that registers radio waves from space – unlike the visible light you see with a normal telescope and your eyes. But one antenna isn’t enough to understand what we’re looking at. Our telescopes make use of interferometry: all the telescopes register these waves, and we combine the signals to create an image.

‘So do we have to turn them all on?’

‘Not all of them, luckily; but the more, the better.’
