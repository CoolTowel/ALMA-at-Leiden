What was that? Something moved very quickly in the corner of his eye. Robin looks at the telescope; but whatever he saw, it’s gone now. 

‘Still here, Robin?’

He snaps awake. ‘Yes, Professor.’

‘Good. Now you should understand the basics of interferometry. Unfortunately, we don’t have time to turn on all of the telescopes and look at your strange object. After all, it’s already getting dark. But I’d say that fifteen antennae should suffice.’

‘Just fifteen?’

‘Just fifteen. And you can help by providing their optimal placement. Shall we take a break while you figure that out?’

‘Of course.’ His mind was still with that strange moving thing.
