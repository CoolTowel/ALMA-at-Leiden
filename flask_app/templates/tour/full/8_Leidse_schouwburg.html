‘…actually, it’s probably not a red giant’, Jeremy concludes after a lengthy monologue.

‘Then what is it?’ Robin asks.

‘It's probably too early to tell. Here at ALMA, we look at all kinds of astronomical objects. Not just stars, but also planets, galaxies, even black holes...’

‘So a planet, maybe?’

‘Not likely! At ALMA, we have made some wonderful images of planets in the past. But we can see them with our naked eye, although they are tiny in the night sky. Your object, like many of the objects we observe at ALMA, is invisible to us. They are too far away, and some don't emit any light, only radio waves. Thanks to these radio telescopes, we can make these invisible objects visible.’

He shows the book to Robin. ‘Look at all these amazing images. Without ALMA, we would never have seen any of this!’
