Robin panted. They had only been walking for an hour, but he feels out of breath already, as if he'd ran a marathon. As they reach the next antenna, he stops and sits down.

‘Are you all right?’ Erica asks, concerned.

‘Yes, it's just... quite tiring,’ Robin says.

Erica sits down next to him. ‘The air is thin up here. We're at 5000 metres altitude, so there's much less oxygen here. If you're not acclimatized, you run out of breath very quickly. Even I have trouble with it sometimes.’

‘Why is ALMA here, anyway?’

‘You see...’ Robin hears little of Erica's explanation. He sees something dart behind the radio telescope. It's the second time already; it can't be a coincidence. Is it the lack of oxygen, or is something going on here?
